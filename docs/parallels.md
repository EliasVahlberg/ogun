
There is significant research and industrial literature drawing parallels between PCB optimization and city planning. Both fields treat their design space as a complex network problem where the goal is to maximize efficiency while adhering to strict spatial and physical constraints. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] 
1. Conceptual Parallels
Experts often describe a PCB as a "micro-city" where components and infrastructure mirror urban elements: [2] 

* Stack-up & Foundation: The PCB layer stack corresponds to the land and structural foundations of a city.
* Components & Buildings: Electrical and mechanical components represent buildings and houses.
* Traces & Roads: Copper traces and vias function as the road networks and intersections that connect everything together. [2] 

2. Algorithmic Overlap
Research in Electronic Design Automation (EDA) and Urban Design Optimization (UDO) frequently utilizes the same mathematical frameworks: [6] 

* Ant Colony Optimization (ACO): Used in city planning to optimize traffic flow and landscape design, it is similarly modified for PCB routing to manage trace lengths and minimize congestion.
* Genetic Algorithms: These are applied to find near-optimal component placement sequences in PCB assembly and to optimize urban architectural characteristics.
* Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF): Originally used for managing robot fleets or city traffic, algorithms like Conflict-Based Search (CBS) have been successfully adapted for high-density PCB routing to minimize vias and improve efficiency.
* Force-Directed Placement: A classical PCB algorithm that models components as masses and nets as springs is mirrored in urban models that use Linear Programming to allocate land use while minimizing conflicts between residential and commercial zones. [7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13] 

3. Digital Twins & Simulation
Modern city planning increasingly relies on Digital Twins—real-time virtual models of urban areas—to simulate scenarios and optimize designs without real-world risk. This directly parallels the use of advanced simulation tools in PCB design to test for Signal Integrity (SI) and Thermal Management before fabrication. [14, 15, 16, 17, 18] 
4. Key Comparative Research

|---|---|
